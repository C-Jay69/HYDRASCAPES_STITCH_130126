1. Fiordo di Furore

Nestled in a deep gorge, this "fjord" is actually a ria—a narrow coastal inlet created by the Schiato stream. It's home to a tiny beach and a collection of old fishermen's houses that seem to cling to the rock faces.

2. Atrani: The Smallest Town in Italy

Just a ten-minute walk from Amalfi, Atrani remains miraculously untouched by mass tourism. Its maze of whitewashed alleys and stairways is a delight for photographers and slow-travel enthusiasts alike.
